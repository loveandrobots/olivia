# 07 · Things to Avoid

## Brand guardrails.

---

These patterns directly contradict Olivia's brand values. If you encounter any of these in a design, feature, or communication — stop and rethink. This list is a commitment to our users.

### Red notification badges or urgent visual indicators
Use Ember dots sparingly and only for genuinely time-sensitive items. Never use red.

### Streak counters, gamification, or engagement metrics
These create anxiety and guilt. Olivia doesn't reward consistency or punish absence.

### Exclamation marks in system communication
Exclamation marks create urgency and false excitement. Use periods. Be calm.

### Sycophantic praise or empty validation
"Great job!" and "You're doing amazing!" are hollow. Just help with the next thing.

### Dense dashboards or information overload
Never show everything at once. Progressive disclosure, always.

### Competitive or comparative language
Never compare the user to others, to averages, or to their past performance.

### Dark patterns or persuasive design
No opt-out tricks, no guilt-based prompts, no manufactured urgency. Ever.

### Bright, saturated, or high-contrast color accents
Stay within the palette. Neon, bright red, electric blue — these violate the calm.

### Auto-playing sounds or haptic buzzing
All sensory feedback should be opt-in, gentle, and easily disabled.

### Language that implies the user is behind or failing
"Overdue," "missed," "falling behind" — replace with neutral, forward-looking framing.

---

> When in doubt, ask: does this make someone's day calmer?
> If not, it doesn't belong in Olivia.
