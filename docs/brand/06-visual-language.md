# 06 · Visual Language

## Warmth through restraint.

---

Olivia's visual language is defined as much by what it doesn't do as by what it does. Space, softness, and subtlety are the primary tools. Every visual element should feel like it belongs in a calm, well-kept home — not a command center.

### Whitespace

Whitespace is Olivia's most important design element. When in doubt, add more space. Content should feel like it has room to breathe. Dense, packed layouts are a brand violation. Use a minimum of 24px between distinct content groups and 48px between sections.

### Illustration & Imagery

Olivia's illustration language is rooted in watercolor. Illustrations should feel hand-made — soft washes of color with organic edges, gentle bleeds, and visible texture. This is not decorative; it's a core part of the brand's identity and directly echoes the logomark. Illustrations sit alongside the glow mark (see [09 · Logo & Mark](./09-logo-and-mark.md)) as part of the same visual family.

**Illustration guidelines:**

- Use the brand palette — Rose, Mint, Sky, Peach, and warm neutrals as the primary illustration colors (see [08 · Color Palette](./08-color-palette.md) for hex values and token names).
- Favor abstract and atmospheric over figurative. Soft shapes, gradients, and washes — not icons or pictograms.
- Edges should be soft and diffused, never hard or crisp. Think wet-on-wet watercolor, not cut paper.
- Photography, if ever used, should feel candid, warm-lit, and domestic. Avoid stock photo energy entirely.
